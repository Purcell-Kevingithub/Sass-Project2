Visit Myanmar

Did you know?
Myanmar was known as Burma until 1989 when the military junta renamed the country Myanmar. The capital, Rangoon, became Yangon.
Did you know?
The mountains in Burma are home to many precious stones. Around 90 percent of the world's rubies come from Burma. Sapphires and jade are also abundant.

Eat delicious food

Did you know?
Burmese cuisine is characterised by extensive use of fish products such as fish sauce, ngapi and dried prawn. Mohinga is the traditional breakfast dish and is Myanmar's national dish
Did you know?
Women (and to a lesser extent, men) wear a yellowish paste called thanaka. The paste cools skin, prevents sun damage, clears up acne and can even reduce fevers and headaches when ingested.

Explore Beautiful Temples

Did you know?
The Shwe U Min Natural Cave Pagoda of Pindaya is a natural cave complex filled with over 8,000 statues or ‘images’ of the Buddha.
Did you know?
Founded in the second century AD, the kingdom once had over 10,000 Buddhist temples, pagodas and monasteries.
